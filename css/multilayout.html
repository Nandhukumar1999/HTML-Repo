Chandrayaan-3 is the third Moon mission by India's space agency ISRO. The goal is to place a lander and rover on the lunar surface and operate them for roughly one lunar day, or 14 Earth days. The small rover, which weighs just 26 kilograms (57 pounds), will fly to the Moon inside the lander.

MSMEs demand release of pending subsidy for machinery

Coimbatore: The Tamil Nadu Association of Cottage and Medium Enterprises (TACT) has sought immediate release of 25% subsidy for machinery that has been pending for more than a year now. The state government offers 25% subsidy on the investment that the micro, small and medium enterprises (MSMEs) make in plant and machinery. The subsidy comes with an upper limit of ₹1.5 crore. The subsidy is given in a go. There is 10% additional subsidy as well for the micro enterprises and that is disbursed in various instalments. J James, district president, TACT, told TOI that around 250 MSME units in the district were yet to get the 25% subsidy. “Since the funds are yet to be released, the units are still paying interest on the loans they took to buy the machinery. Therefore, we are requesting the government to release the funds to save us from further financial burden.”

CM launches one crore tree planting drive in Ramnad

RAMANATHAPURAM: Chief minister M K Stalin launched one crore tree planting drive in Ramanathapuram on Friday. Under the chief miniser’s aspirational Green Tamil Nadu project to improve the green cover of the state one crore tree saplings are going to be planted. Various departments are going to raise the saplings under this project and tree species like East Indian Walnut, Gooseberry, Bamboo, Banyan, Peepul, Fig, Manila Tamarind, wood apple, guava, bendy are raised for the purpose. Marking the inaugural of the project, Stalin distributed tree saplings to the public. The tree-planting drive will be completed by January 2024. The chief minister also felicitated 10 government officials and five non-governmental organizations for their social service. Thondi town panchayat tax collector S Mangalanatha Sethupathi, Pattanamkatthan panchayat conservancy worker Udayar, Pamakudi municipality driver Rajendran, Pappanenthal Panchayat Union School headmistress Saravana Boopathy, Ramanathapuram Government Hospital Nurse Bhuvani, Panithavayal anganwadi worker Reeta, Rameswaram fisheries superintendent Nadesh Babu, Keelakarai Panchayat Assistant Pandi, Rameswwaram Traffic Head Constable Yuvaraj, Kamudhi Fire and Rescue department staff Palani were given trophies for their social service beyond their regular duty.
